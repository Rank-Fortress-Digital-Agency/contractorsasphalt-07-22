10 asphalt maintenance tips for a nice-looking driveway.


Maintaining your driveway doesn’t have to be a daunting task. Following these ten simple tips, you can keep your driveway looking great for years.

Read Post

1. Seal your driveway

Sealing your driveway is one of the best ways to protect it from the elements and prevent cracking and other damage. You should fill your driveway every one to three years, depending on the climate in your area and the condition of your driveway. Sealing your driveway is a relatively simple process, but it does require some preparation and special equipment.

Before you begin, sweep or pressure wash your driveway to remove any dirt, dust, or debris. Once it’s clean, let it dry completely. Next, fill any cracks or holes with an asphalt patch or sealant. Once the patch or sealant is dry, you’re ready to begin sealing.

2. Remove stains promptly

Stains from oil, gasoline, and other substances can damage your driveway if they’re not removed promptly. Use a degreaser or another type of cleaner specifically designed for removing these types of stains.

Be sure to rinse the area thoroughly after cleaning it. You may need to repeat the process a few times to remove the stain. If the stain is particularly stubborn, you may need a power washer. Be careful not to damage the concrete while using a power washer.

3. Repair cracks as soon as they appear.

Cracks in your driveway can allow water to seep in and cause further damage. So, it’s essential to repair them as soon as possible. You can use a patching compound designed to repair cracks in sphalt driveways.

To repair a crack in your driveway:


  1. Clean the area around the crack with a wire brush and remove any loose debris.
  2. Fill the crack with the patching compound, using a putty knife to spread it evenly.
  3. Smooth the surface of the compound with the putty knife.
  4. Allow the compound to dry according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  5. Once the compound is dry, seal the area with a layer of asphalt sealant.
  6. Allow the sealant to dry according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

4. Fill potholes immediately

Potholes can not only damage your driveway, but they can also be dangerous. So, it’s essential to fill them in as soon as you notice them. You can use a cold patch mix or another type of asphalt repair product to fill the hole. Be sure to smooth the surface, so it’s level with the rest of your driveway.

If you have a lot of potholes, you may need to consult with a professional to repair your driveway. They can assess the damage and make the necessary repairs. In some cases, they may even recommend repaving the entire driveway.

5. Sweep regularly

Sweeping your driveway regularly will help prevent the buildup of dirt and debris. This will also help reduce the risk of staining and damage to your driveway.

When sweeping your driveway, use a soft-bristled brush to avoid scratching the surface. Also, clean the grain direction to avoid damaging the finish.

Sweeping your driveway regularly is essential to keep it looking its best. Following these simple tips can help keep your driveway clean and reduce the risk of damage.

6. power wash periodically

Power washing your driveway periodically will remove any build-up of dirt, grime, and other substances. This will help keep it looking clean and new and can also help to extend its lifespan.

If you have a concrete driveway, power washing is essential. Concrete is a very porous material that can easily absorb stains and dirt. Power washing will remove any deep-seated dirt, grime, and surface-level stains. This will leave your driveway looking clean and new.

8. Don’t use salt

Salt can damage your driveway and hasten the deterioration of the asphalt. So, it’s best to avoid using it altogether. If you must use salt, be sure to use it sparingly and wash it off as soon as possible.

If you have a concrete driveway, you’re in luck. Salt won’t damage concrete the way it does asphalt. However, it can still cause problems. For one, salt can make your concrete slippery, a hazard for anyone walking on it. Additionally, salt can cause your concrete to pit and flake. To avoid these problems, use salt sparingly and wash it off as soon as possible.

9. Be careful with chemicals

Chemicals such as antifreeze and oil can damage your driveway if they spill. So, be sure to clean up any spills immediately. And, if you must store these kinds of chemicals in your garage, be sure to do so safely and securely.

Another potential hazard to your driveway is salt. During the winter, many people use salt to melt ice and snow on their driveways. However, salt can damage concrete and asphalt over time. So, if you use salt on your driveway, brush it off as soon as possible. And try to use a salt alternative when possible.

Ensure your driveway is regularly inspected and repaired as needed. Over time, all driveways will suffer from wear and tear. By having your driveway inspected and repaired regularly, you can help to extend its lifespan.
